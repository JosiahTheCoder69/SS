What was the primary reason for Germany's economic collapse after World War I?

Reparations imposed by the Treaty of Versailles.

What significant event in 1923 exemplified Germany's economic crisis?

The hyperinflation crisis.

What were some social impacts of Germany's economic chaos?

Increased unemployment and civil unrest.

How did Germany's economic turmoil affect other countries?

It triggered a worldwide depression.

What event marked the beginning of the Great Depression in the U.S.?

The stock market crash of October 1929.

What was one major cause of the Great Depression?

Over-speculation in the stock market.

What was a significant consequence of the Great Depression on global economies?

The closure of U.S. banks caused bank runs in other nations.

What were key provisions of the Treaty of Versailles imposed on Germany?

Full responsibility for the war and reparations.

How did many Germans perceive the Treaty of Versailles?

As a Diktat (dictated peace).

What did the harsh terms of the Treaty of Versailles contribute to in Germany?

The rise of political extremism.
