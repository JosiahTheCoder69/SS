Unit 8: Canada’s Environment, Economy, and Government

Canada’s Environmental Issues

Learning Target

Students will be able to identify and describe Canada’s three major environmental issues.

1. Pollution of the Great Lakes

Pollution in the Great Lakes is caused by waste dumping, run-off, and acid rain. Industrial waste and sewage harm aquatic life, and toxic chemicals can cause serious health problems in humans.

2. Mining in the Canadian Shield

The Canadian Shield is rich in minerals like gold, silver, and nickel. However, mining contaminates soil and water, harming ecosystems and human health.

3. Deforestation of Timber

Canada’s Boreal Forest is one of the oldest forests in the world. Over-harvesting for paper and industry threatens animal habitats and contributes to environmental damage.

Canada’s Economy

Learning Target

Students will be able to answer Canada’s basic economic questions.

Canada’s Economic System

Canada has a **mixed economy**, leaning closer to a market system. Private businesses make most decisions, but the government controls some industries, like healthcare.

Specialization

Canada specializes in **energy, agriculture, and manufacturing**. It is the U.S.’s largest energy supplier and one of the world’s top wheat and grain producers.

Natural Resources

  • Energy: Canada produces oil, natural gas, coal, and hydroelectric power.
  • Agriculture: A major supplier of wheat, grain, and other crops.
  • Manufacturing: Canada produces as many cars as the U.S.

Trade

Canada exports resources like energy to the U.S. Over-specialization can make a country too dependent, so diversification is important.

Canada’s Government

Learning Target

Students will be able to explain how Canada’s government works.

Government Structure

Canada is a **parliamentary democracy, a constitutional monarchy, and a federation**. Citizens elect Members of Parliament, who choose the Prime Minister.

Parliament

Canada’s **legislative branch** is called Parliament. It has two parts:

  • House of Commons: Elected by citizens.
  • Senate: Appointed members.

Branches of Government

  • Executive Branch: Led by the Prime Minister.
  • Legislative Branch: Includes Parliament (House of Commons & Senate).
  • Judicial Branch: Consists of Canada’s court system.

Role of Citizens

Canadians vote for Members of Parliament and provincial leaders. They also participate in referendums, like Quebec’s independence votes.
