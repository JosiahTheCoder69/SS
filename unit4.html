Unit 4: Latin America’s Culture and Geography

Introduction to Latin America

Latin America includes countries in the Western Hemisphere where Romance languages (Spanish, Portuguese, and French) are spoken. The region comprises four main areas:

  • North America: Mexico
  • Central America: Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama
  • Caribbean: Cuba, Dominican Republic, Haiti, Puerto Rico, Jamaica, Bahamas, and others
  • South America: Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Peru, Venezuela, and more

Languages of Latin America

  • Spanish: The primary language in most of Latin America due to Spanish colonization.
  • Portuguese: Spoken primarily in Brazil, a legacy of Portuguese colonization.
  • French: Spoken in Haiti, French Guiana, and parts of the Caribbean.
  • Indigenous Languages: Still spoken by Indigenous communities, including Quechua, Nahuatl, and Guarani.

Religion in Latin America

  • Catholicism: The dominant religion, brought by Spanish and Portuguese colonizers.
  • Indigenous Beliefs: Persist in some regions alongside Catholicism.

Ethnic Groups in Latin America

  • European Descent: Primarily Spanish, Portuguese, and French ancestry.
  • Indigenous Peoples: Includes groups like the Maya, Aztec, and Inca.
  • African Descent: Descendants of enslaved Africans, particularly in Haiti, Jamaica, and Brazil.
  • Mestizo: Mixed European and Indigenous ancestry, common in Mexico and Central America.
  • Mulatto: Mixed African and European ancestry, prevalent in Caribbean nations.

Countries of Central America

  • Guatemala: Known for jade, coffee, and a large Indigenous population. Capital: Guatemala City.
  • El Salvador: Smallest Central American country, known for coffee. Capital: San Salvador.
  • Honduras: Exports bananas and coffee, known for Mayan ruins. Capital: Tegucigalpa.
  • Nicaragua: Known for lakes, volcanoes, and coffee. Capital: Managua.
  • Costa Rica: Known for biodiversity, eco-tourism, and stable government. Capital: San José.
  • Panama: Known for the Panama Canal, connecting Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. Capital: Panama City.

Countries of the Caribbean

  • Cuba: Largest island in the Caribbean, known for music and cultural history. Capital: Havana.
  • Dominican Republic: Shares Hispaniola with Haiti, known for tourism and cocoa. Capital: Santo Domingo.
  • Haiti: Known for coffee, located on Hispaniola. Capital: Port-au-Prince.
  • Puerto Rico: A U.S. territory with a mix of American and Latin cultures. Capital: San Juan.
  • Jamaica: Known for reggae music, coffee, and vibrant culture. Capital: Kingston.
  • Bahamas: Composed of hundreds of islands, known for tourism. Capital: Nassau.
  • French Guiana: A French overseas department, known for the Guiana Space Centre. Capital: Cayenne.
  • Barbados: Known for tourism and beautiful beaches. Capital: Bridgetown.
  • Trinidad and Tobago: Known for oil, natural gas, and Carnival. Capital: Port of Spain.

Countries of South America

  • Brazil: The largest country, known for the Amazon rainforest and Carnival. Capital: Brasília.
  • Argentina: Known for beef, wine, and tango music. Capital: Buenos Aires.
  • Colombia: Famous for coffee and flowers. Capital: Bogotá.
  • Venezuela: Rich in oil resources. Capital: Caracas.
  • Ecuador: Known for the Galapagos Islands. Capital: Quito.
  • Peru: Known for its Inca heritage and potatoes. Capital: Lima.
  • Chile: Known for wine, mining, and a long Pacific coastline. Capital: Santiago.
  • Paraguay: Known for agriculture and hydroelectric power. Capital: Asunción.
  • Uruguay: Known for beaches, beef exports, and a stable democracy. Capital: Montevideo.
  • Bolivia: Known for indigenous culture and mineral resources. Capital: La Paz (administrative) and Sucre (constitutional).
  • Guyana: English-speaking, known for rainforests and waterfalls. Capital: Georgetown.
  • Suriname: Known for cultural diversity and rainforest. Capital: Paramaribo.
