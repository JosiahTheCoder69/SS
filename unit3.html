Unit 3: World War I, The Great Depression, and The Holocaust

The Treaty of Versailles

After World War I, the Treaty of Versailles placed harsh reparations on Germany, leading to economic depression and setting the stage for further conflict.

  • Responsibility: Germany was forced to accept full responsibility for the war.
  • Reparations: Germany was required to pay huge sums of money to the Allies.
  • Military: Severe restrictions were placed on the size of the German military.
  • Loss of Land: Germany lost significant territories and all its colonies.

The Global Depression

The worldwide depression affected economies across Europe and the United States:

  • The U.S. fell into the Great Depression in 1929 after the stock market crash.
  • Germany faced hyperinflation and widespread poverty due to reparations.
  • The interconnectedness of global economies meant that the collapse of one major economy affected others.

The Holocaust

The Holocaust was the genocide of around six million Jews by the Nazi regime and their collaborators during World War II. This event, also known as the Shoah, aimed to eliminate the entire Jewish community of Europe.

  • Death Squads: Einsatzgruppen, or Nazi death squads, murdered Jews in Eastern Europe.
  • Extermination Camps: Jews were transported to camps where many perished.
  • Other Targeted Groups: Roma, disabled individuals, Soviet POWs, and others were also persecuted.
  • Collaborators: Countries such as Lithuania, Ukraine, and Hungary collaborated with Nazi Germany.

Aftermath of the Holocaust

After WWII, the horrors of the Holocaust became known to the world. The Allies conducted war crimes trials to bring justice to the perpetrators. One of the most significant consequences of the Holocaust was the establishment of Israel as a homeland for the Jewish people in 1947.

Antisemitism and Its History

Antisemitism is hostility, prejudice, or discrimination against Jews. This section explores antisemitism throughout history and the rich cultures that were lost due to the Holocaust.
