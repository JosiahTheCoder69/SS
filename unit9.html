Unit 9: Australia's Geography, History, and Government

Australia's Geography

Learning Target

Students will be able to locate and identify major geographical features of Australia.

Where is Australia?

Australia is both a country and a continent located in the Southern Hemisphere. It is surrounded by the Pacific Ocean and the Indian Ocean.

Major Geographical Features

  • Coral Sea: A part of the Pacific Ocean, located northeast of Australia.
  • Great Barrier Reef: The world's largest coral reef system, home to diverse marine life.
  • Uluru (Ayers Rock): A massive sandstone monolith, sacred to the Indigenous people.
  • Great Victoria Desert: A vast desert covering parts of Western and South Australia.

Climate Zones

  • Tropical: Found in the north, experiencing high temperatures and rainfall.
  • Arid: The central Outback regions, with very little rainfall.
  • Temperate: Southeastern and southwestern Australia, with moderate climate and seasons.

Australia's History

Learning Target

Students will be able to explain the impact of European settlement on the Indigenous people of Australia.

European Exploration and Colonization

  • 1770: British Captain James Cook claimed Australia for Great Britain.
  • 1788: The First Fleet arrived, establishing a penal colony in Sydney.
  • By 1852, over 150,000 convicts had been transported to Australia.

Impact on Aboriginal Australians

  • Many Aboriginal tribes were displaced as the British settled along the coast.
  • Diseases like smallpox wiped out large portions of the population.
  • British settlers had superior weapons, leading to violent conflicts.

Australia Becomes a Nation

  • 1901: Australia became an independent country.
  • Policies limited where Aboriginal Australians could live and work.
  • By the 1960s, Australia began recognizing Indigenous rights.

Australia's Government

Learning Target

Students will be able to explain the structure and functions of Australia's government.

Government Structure

  • Constitutional Monarchy: The King of England is the Head of State.
  • Parliamentary Democracy: The Prime Minister is the Head of Government.
  • Federal System: Australia is divided into states and territories.

Branches of Government

  • Executive: Led by the Prime Minister and the Cabinet.
  • Legislative: Parliament consists of the House of Representatives and the Senate.
  • Judicial: The High Court interprets laws.

Key Facts

  • Australia is a former British colony and part of the British Commonwealth.
  • The King of England (currently King Charles III) is the ceremonial Head of State.
  • Each state and territory governs itself but must follow national laws.

Role of Citizens

  • Australians vote for members of Parliament.
  • They elect state and local government officials.
  • Voting is mandatory by law in Australia.
