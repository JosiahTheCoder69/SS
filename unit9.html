Unit 9: Australia's Geography, History, and Government

Australia's Geography

Learning Target

Students will be able to locate and identify major geographical features of Australia.

Where is Australia?

Australia is a country and a continent located in the Southern Hemisphere. It is surrounded by the Pacific Ocean and the Indian Ocean.

Major Geographical Features

  • Coral Sea: Part of the Pacific Ocean, located northeast of Australia.
  • Great Barrier Reef: The world's largest coral reef system, home to diverse marine life.
  • Uluru (Ayers Rock): A massive sandstone monolith, sacred to the Indigenous people.
  • Great Victoria Desert: A vast desert covering parts of Western and South Australia.

Australia's History

Learning Target

Students will be able to explain the impact of European settlement on the Indigenous people of Australia.

Indigenous Australians

Aboriginal Australians have lived on the continent for over 65,000 years. They have a deep connection to the land and rich cultural traditions.

European Exploration and Colonization

  • 1770: British Sea Captain James Cook explored Australia and claimed it for Britain.
  • 1788: The First Fleet arrived at Sydney, establishing Australia as a penal colony.
  • By 1852, there were over 150,000 convicts in Australia.
  • Eventually, free settlers arrived, and Australia expanded.

Impact on the Aboriginal People

  • The British settled in coastal areas, displacing Indigenous Australians.
  • Many Aboriginal people died from diseases brought by Europeans.
  • Europeans had superior weapons, which led to conflicts and massacres.
  • Some Aboriginal tribes, like those in Tasmania, were completely wiped out.

Australia Becomes a Nation

  • 1901: Australia became an independent country.
  • Government policies attempted to erase Aboriginal culture by restricting where they could live and work.
  • In the 1960s, Australia began recognizing Indigenous rights, but very few Aboriginal people remain today.

Australia's Government

Learning Target

Students will be able to explain the structure and functions of Australia's government.

Government Structure

  • Constitutional Monarchy: The King of England is the Head of State.
  • Parliamentary Democracy: The Prime Minister is the Head of Government.
  • Federal System: Australia is divided into states and territories, each with its own government.

Branches of Government

  • Executive Branch: Led by the Prime Minister and the Cabinet.
  • Legislative Branch: Parliament consists of the House of Representatives and the Senate.
  • Judicial Branch: The High Court and other courts interpret laws.

Key Facts

  • Australia is a former British colony and part of the British Commonwealth.
  • The Head of State is King Charles III.
  • Australia’s government is similar to a constitutional monarchy.
  • Each state and territory governs itself but must follow national laws.

Role of Citizens

  • Australians vote for members of Parliament.
  • They elect state and local government officials.
  • Voting is compulsory in Australia, meaning citizens must vote by law.
